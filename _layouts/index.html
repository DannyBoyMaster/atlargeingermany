On 27th April 1942, Flight Lieutenants Gerald Williams and Hugh Keartland crawled underneath the front gate of a prisoner of war camp in Warburg, Germany.

Their goal, Switzerland, lay to the south. Walking on the autobahn at night, and with limited food and clothing, they covered 450km over 17 days before their eventual re-capture in the Black Forest. By their estimate, the Swiss border was just 3 days away.

Thanks to a report written by Williams shortly after re-capture, we are able to plot their path with a high degree of accuracy.

“At Large In Germany” is an attempt at walking in their footsteps: to retrace Williams and Keartland’s route in the same number of days, at the same time of year, 77 years later.
